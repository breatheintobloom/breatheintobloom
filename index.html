Meet Oxana
Registered Nurse Nursing Manager Somatic Practitioner Breathwork Facilitator Access Bars Somatic Educator

A whole person
waiting to exhale.

For most of her life, Oxana believed strength meant carrying more.

Born in Kazakhstan and raised in Vancouver, she learned early how to adapt, achieve, and persevere. As a university basketball captain, a nurse, and later a healthcare leader, she became someone others could rely on — steady under pressure, capable in a crisis, and always willing to give more of herself.

But behind that strength was a pattern she would come to know well.

“At nineteen, she was diagnosed with psoriasis — an autoimmune condition closely linked to stress. Years later, she would come to see it differently. Not as something that happened to her, but as one of the first signals that her body was asking for a different way of living.”

After more than a decade in healthcare, and following a year spent traveling and rebuilding her life abroad, Oxana returned with a deeper understanding of something she had witnessed both personally and professionally: the people who care for everyone else are often the ones receiving the least care themselves.

Today, she brings together her background in nursing, leadership, breathwork, and somatic practice to create spaces where people can step out of survival mode, reconnect with themselves, and remember that wellbeing is not something that must be earned.

“Breathe Into Bloom was born from the belief that beneath the pressure, responsibility, and constant demand to keep going, there is already a whole person waiting to exhale.”

Clinical depth. 10+ years as a nurse. I understand bodies, nervous systems, and what high-stakes environments do to the people inside them.
Somatic training. Certified Breathwork Facilitator and somatic practitioner — two-year embodiment mentorship, Access Bars. Embodied practice, not theory.
The translator. I speak both languages — clinical and somatic — which means your team doesn’t have to choose between evidence and experience.
Founded Breathe Into Bloom to serve the holders — those who give the most and are offered the least space to receive.
